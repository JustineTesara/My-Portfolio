Improve Accessibility — Desktop Icons
index.html Accessibility
Why does this matter? Your desktop icons use <div> tags, but they behave like buttons (you can click them). Screen readers (used by visually impaired users) don't know these divs are clickable. The fix is to add role="button" and tabindex="0" — this tells browsers and assistive technology "this thing is interactive." It also makes keyboard navigation work (pressing Tab moves to icons, Enter activates them).
1
Open index.html and find the class="desktop-icon" divs (starting around line 14).
2
Add role="button", tabindex="0", and an aria-label to each one as shown below.
3
There are 7 icons total — repeat this change for each one.
❌ Before — plain div, not accessible
✅ After — accessible interactive element
14<div class="desktop-icon" data-window="about">
15 <!-- ❌ Screen readers can't tell this is clickable -->
16 <div class="icon-image">...</div>
17 <span class="icon-label">About Me</span>
18</div>
14<div
15 class="desktop-icon"
16 data-window="about"
17 role="button" <!-- Tells screen readers it's clickable -->
18 tabindex="0" <!-- Makes it keyboard-focusable -->

20>
21 <div class="icon-image">...</div>
22 <span class="icon-label">About Me</span>
